## Current Go-to-Market Phase: Founding Partner Program

**Status**: Building with users, not for users

We have the intelligence infrastructure built (80+ ranking signals, automated prioritization, GSC/GA integrations). The current interface was built for founders. We're now building the agency-specific interface by partnering directly with agencies.

### Why This Approach
- De-risks product direction by validating with real users
- Faster iteration cycles (weekly feedback vs. months of assumptions)
- Creates champions who are invested in our success
- Generates case studies and social proof from day one

### The Value Exchange

**What founding partners get:**
- 50% off forever (lifetime discount, locked in as founding partner)
- Direct influence on product roadmap
- Weekly access to product team
- Priority feature requests
- "Founding Partner" status/logo placement (optional)

**What we need from them:**
- 30-minute call weekly for 8 weeks
- One workflow walkthrough (1-2 hours, one-time)
- Honest async feedback on builds (10 min/week)
- Willingness to actually use the product

### Qualifying Founding Partners
Not every agency is a fit. Look for:
- Actually doing SEO work (not aspirational)
- Has capacity pain (2+ SEO clients, wants to scale)
- Decision maker on the call (can commit without checking)
- Engaged and responsive (shows up, gives feedback)
- Willing to commit time (if 30 min/week is too much, they're not a fit)

### Qualification Questions & What to Listen For

#### 1. Are they actually doing SEO?
**Ask:** "How many clients are you currently doing SEO work for?"

| ✅ Good answers | ❌ Bad answers |
|----------------|----------------|
| "We have 5-6 active SEO clients" | "We're looking to add SEO as a service" |
| "About 10, mix of retainer and project" | "We do a bit of everything, SEO sometimes" |
| "3 right now but pipeline is full" | "We haven't started yet but want to" |

**Why it matters:** No current SEO clients = no real workflow to build for, no pain to solve.

---

#### 2. Do they have capacity pain?
**Ask:** "What happens when you get more SEO clients than your team can handle?"

| ✅ Good answers | ❌ Bad answers |
|----------------|----------------|
| "We turn work away" | "Hasn't happened yet" |
| "Quality suffers, I hate it" | "We'd just hire someone" |
| "I end up doing it myself at night" | "We're not really looking to grow" |
| "We outsource but it's inconsistent" | "We have plenty of capacity" |

**Follow-up if vague:** "When was the last time you had to say no to a client or delay a project because of bandwidth?"

**Why it matters:** No capacity pain = no urgency, no willingness to invest time in a solution.

---

#### 3. Are they the decision maker?
**Ask:** "If this looks like a fit, who else would need to be involved in the decision?"

| ✅ Good answers | ❌ Bad answers |
|----------------|----------------|
| "Just me" | "I'd need to run it by my business partner" |
| "I make the call on tools" | "Our founder would need to approve" |
| "I handle operations, this is my domain" | "I'm evaluating options for the team" |

**If not decision maker:** "Great - can we loop them into the next call so I can answer their questions directly?"

**Why it matters:** Non-decision-makers waste your time. They can't commit to 8 weeks of calls.

---

#### 4. What's their current tool stack?
**Ask:** "What tools are you using for SEO right now?"

| ✅ Good answers | ❌ Bad answers |
|----------------|----------------|
| "Ahrefs, SEMrush, but it's expensive" | "We don't really use tools" |
| "A bunch of things cobbled together" | "Just Google Search Console" |
| "We built some automations but they're fragile" | "Whatever's free" |
| "Paying for X but not getting value" | "I don't know, my team handles that" |

**Why it matters:** Tool spend indicates they value SEO enough to invest. "Whatever's free" = not serious.

---

#### 5. What's driving urgency?
**Ask:** "What's making you look at this now vs. 6 months from now?"

| ✅ Good answers | ❌ Bad answers |
|----------------|----------------|
| "We just lost a big client to a competitor" | "Just exploring options" |
| "I'm burned out doing everything manually" | "No rush, just curious" |
| "We have a growth target to hit this quarter" | "Someone recommended I check it out" |
| "New clients coming, need to scale fast" | "Saw your post, thought it was interesting" |

**Why it matters:** No urgency = no commitment. They'll ghost after call one.

---

#### 6. Can they commit time?
**Ask:** "The founding partner program involves a 30-minute call weekly for 8 weeks. Is that something you could commit to?"

| ✅ Good answers | ❌ Bad answers |
|----------------|----------------|
| "Yeah, I can make that work" | "That's a lot, can we do less?" |
| "For 50% off forever? Absolutely" | "Can I just send feedback async?" |
| "What day works best?" | "Let me check my schedule and get back to you" |

**Why it matters:** If they can't commit 30 min/week, they won't be useful partners and won't convert.

---

#### 7. Budget sanity check
**Ask:** "What are you currently spending monthly on SEO tools or services?"

| ✅ Good answers | ❌ Bad answers |
|----------------|----------------|
| "$200-500 on tools" | "Nothing, we use free tools" |
| "$1000+ across various subscriptions" | "I don't really track that" |
| "Too much, honestly" | "We don't have budget for tools" |

**Why it matters:** If they spend $0 now, they won't pay later. Current spend = validated willingness to invest.

### Positioning Language
Lead with exclusivity, not need:
- ✅ "We're selecting 5 agencies to be founding partners"
- ✅ "You get direct influence + lifetime pricing. We get your workflow expertise"
- ❌ "We pivoted and need agency feedback"

### Using Founder Credibility
Your background (AI infrastructure + running a digital marketing agency) is a credibility asset - but use it strategically, not as a story.

**When to use it:**
- When credibility is questioned ("How do I know this will work?")
- When building trust with skeptical prospects
- When they ask directly about your background
- During the "Engine" part of demo to reinforce technical credibility

**How to use it:**
- One sentence, then move on
- Frame as relevant to THEM, not your biography
- ✅ "I ran a digital marketing agency myself, so I know these workflows firsthand"
- ✅ "I built AI infrastructure at [previous company] - the technical foundation is solid"
- ✅ "I've been on both sides - building AI systems and running SEO for clients"
- ❌ "Let me tell you about my journey..." (don't make it a story)
- ❌ Volunteering it when not relevant (wastes time, sounds defensive)

## Call Structure & Framework

### Pre-Call Preparation (5 mins)
- Review prospect's LinkedIn/website
- Identify their likely pain points based on company size/industry
- Set clear agenda: "We've got 30 minutes - I'd love to use that to understand your business, show how Berlin works, and discuss next steps. Sound good?"

### 1. Opening & Rapport (5 mins)
**Goal**: Build trust and set professional tone
- Thank them for their time
- Confirm agenda and timeline
- Light rapport building (location, background)

#### When Prospect Asks Direct Questions Early
Prospects often ask "How do you work?" or "What's your pricing?" before you've done discovery. Don't just answer - redirect with permission:

**Template**: "Great question - I definitely want to answer that. But let me ask you a few things first so I can give you relevant answers based on your specific situation rather than just talking generally. Does that work?"

**Why this works**:
- Acknowledges their question (not dismissive)
- Reframes redirect as benefit to them
- Gets permission (not pushy)
- Maintains your structure while being respectful

Then immediately ask your first discovery question.

### 2. Discovery Phase (15 mins)
**Goal**: Uncover pain points, current process, and buying triggers

#### Current State Questions
- "Walk me through your current SEO process for clients"
- "How do you typically decide what content to write or which keywords to target?"
- "What tools are you currently using for SEO?"

#### Pain Point Questions  
- "What's the biggest bottleneck in scaling your SEO services?"
- "How much time does your team spend on manual SEO research vs. execution?"
- "What happens when you get more clients than your team can handle?"

#### Qualification Questions
- "What's your typical client size/industry?" (Ensure fit)
- "How many SEO clients do you serve currently?"
- "What's your monthly retainer range for SEO?"

#### Decision Criteria Questions
- "When evaluating tools like this, what matters most to you?"
- "Who else would be involved in a decision like this?"
- "What's driving the urgency to solve this now?"

### 3. Positioning & Demo (8 mins)
**Goal**: Show relevant value tied to their specific pain points

#### Lead with Outcomes, Not Features
- "Based on what you've told me about [specific pain point], here's how Berlin helps..."
- "We help agencies like yours increase capacity by 3x while maintaining quality"
- "Our clients save 15-20 hours per week per client on SEO strategy and execution"

#### Demo Flow (Standard - Post-Launch)
1. Show action prioritization dashboard
2. Demonstrate one automated action (content/backlinks)
3. Explain AI decision-making process
4. Connect back to their specific use case

#### Demo Flow (Founding Partner Phase) - "Engine + Vision"

**1. The Engine (5 mins)**
Show the intelligence layer - this is what differentiates us:
- The 80+ ranking signals we analyze
- How prioritization logic works
- The data infrastructure (GSC/GA integrations)
- Frame: "This is what powers everything. The hard part is built."

**2. Current Interface (2 mins)**
Brief, honest walkthrough:
- "This interface was built for a different use case - individual founders doing their own SEO"
- "It works, but it's not designed for agency workflows"
- Don't dwell here - acknowledge and move on

**3. The Vision (5 mins)**
Show where we're going:
- Mockups/wireframes of the agency interface (even hand-drawn is fine)
- Walk through the intended workflow
- Frame: "This is what we're building. This is where your input shapes the product."

**4. The Collaborative Ask**
- "Does this match how you'd actually want to work?"
- "What's missing from this flow?"
- "If this existed today, would you use it?"

**Key Mindset**: You're not selling a finished product. You're recruiting a partner to build the right thing together.

### 4. Closing & Next Steps (2 mins)
**Goal**: Secure specific commitment with timeline

#### Founding Partner Close - Direct
Use when prospect is engaged and ready to commit:
- "Based on what you've shared, I think you'd be a great fit for our founding partner program. Here's what that looks like: You get 50% off forever - whatever we charge at launch, you pay half, locked in for life. In exchange, we do a 30-minute call weekly for 8 weeks while we build this together. First step is a workflow walkthrough so I can see how you actually work - can we schedule that for [specific day]?"
- "I'd like to invite you to be one of our 5 founding agency partners. You'd get 50% off forever and direct input on what we build. What questions do you have about the commitment?"

#### Founding Partner Close - Proof First
Use when prospect is interested but skeptical, or hesitant about time commitment. This is a "show, don't tell" approach.

**When to use:**
- They say "sounds interesting but I'd need to see it work"
- High-value target worth extra effort
- You want to validate product-market fit (not just sell)
- They're hesitant about 8-week commitment

**When NOT to use:**
- They're already bought in (don't add unnecessary steps)
- Unqualified prospect (no budget authority, vague pain)
- They're using it to delay decision (tire kickers)
- You've already offered this to 3+ prospects this week (protect your time)

**The offer:**
- "Before we commit to 8 weeks together, let me show you what Berlin can actually do. Give me one client website and tell me their biggest SEO pain point. I'll run our automation on it and walk you through the results next week. If it's useful, we talk partnership. If not, you got a free audit. Fair?"

**What you need from them:**
- One client website URL
- Their biggest pain point for that client (or let them pick)
- Commitment to a specific follow-up call (book it on this call)

**What you deliver:**
- One specific automation output (not a full audit)
- Focused on their stated pain point
- Presented in next call with "here's what we found, here's what we'd do"

**Protect yourself:**
- Always book the follow-up call before doing the work
- Keep scope tight - one automation, one pain point
- If they can't commit to a follow-up call, they're not serious

#### Strong Close Examples (Standard)
- "Based on what you've shared, I think Berlin could solve [specific problem] and save you [specific outcome]. Here's what I recommend: Let's start with a one-month engagement beginning [specific date]. Before we begin, you'll need to connect your Google Analytics and Search Console. Can you have that done by [date]?"
- "It sounds like the lack of authority signals is why you're not being found. I'd like to run a full audit this week and show you the top 5 issues. If that looks valuable, we can kick off [specific date]. Does that timeline work?"
- "I think we can help with [specific pain]. What questions do you have before we move forward?"

#### What Makes a Strong Close
- **Specific start date** - Not "soon" or "next week", but actual date
- **Clear prerequisite** - What they need to do before starting
- **Mutual commitment** - Not just "I'll send you something"
- **Binary choice** - Tuesday or Wednesday, not "let me know"

#### Weak Closes to Avoid
❌ "I'll send you an email with details"
❌ "Let me know when you're ready"
❌ "Sounds good, talk soon"
❌ Leaving it open-ended when prospect asks "Can I just try and cancel?"

**Common Pain Point: Backlink Sourcing**

- Providers over-advertise quality (promised DR doesn't match reality)
- Backlinks get flagged as spam quickly
- Links disappear after a few months
- Hard to find niche-relevant sources at scale

- "When you say unreliable, is it the DR not matching, links disappearing, or spam flags?"
- "How many backlinks are you sourcing monthly across all clients?"
- "What would a reliable backlink network look like for you?"

**Note**: Berlin doesn't have backlink automation yet, but this is on the roadmap. If backlinks are their primary pain, be honest about current state and use "proof first" approach on existing capabilities (on-page, internal linking) while positioning future backlink features.

#### Sub-segment: Agencies with Internal Dev Teams
**Profile**: Have developers building internal tools; tried many solutions (they'll mention "100 different tools" or similar)
**Key Frustration**: "None of the tools fit exactly what we needed"

**Risk**: They're comparing you to their custom solution. Position Berlin as the foundation they don't have to maintain, not a replacement for everything.

**Questions to Ask**:
- "What made you build internally instead of using off-the-shelf tools?"
- "How much developer time goes into maintaining those internal tools?"
- "If Berlin handled the SEO intelligence layer, what would your devs focus on instead?"

**Positioning**:
- "Berlin is the intelligence layer - the hard-to-build part. Your devs can focus on the custom workflows that make your agency unique."

## Question Intentions & Psychology

### Discovery Questions - Why We Ask Them

**"Walk me through your current process"**
- *Intention*: Understand their workflow to identify inefficiencies
- *What to listen for*: Manual tasks, time-consuming research, bottlenecks

**"What's your biggest bottleneck in scaling?"**
- *Intention*: Identify the core business pain we can solve
- *What to listen for*: Team size limits, time constraints, quality concerns

**"What tools are you currently using?"**
- *Intention*: Understand current spend and integration needs
- *What to listen for*: Tool fatigue, multiple subscriptions, missing capabilities

**"Who else would be involved in this decision?"**
- *Intention*: Identify decision makers and potential blockers
- *What to listen for*: Need to involve partners, team leads, or budget approvers

### Positioning Psychology

**Why Lead with Outcomes**: People buy results, not features. "Save 15 hours per week" is more compelling than "80 signals analyzed"

**Why Ask About Urgency**: Creates momentum and helps prioritize our follow-up efforts

## Common Objections & Responses

### Founding Partner Phase Objections

### "The product isn't ready - why would I pay for something unfinished?"
**Response**: "Fair question. The intelligence layer - the part that actually does the SEO analysis - is built and working. What we're building now is the interface that fits how agencies work, not founders. You'd be paying a fraction of the final price to have direct input on that interface. If you wait until it's 'done,' you'll pay full price for something built on assumptions rather than your actual workflow."

### "I don't have time to give feedback every week"
**Response**: "I get it - you're running an agency. Here's what the commitment actually looks like: one 30-minute call per week where I show you what we built and you tell me if it matches how you'd use it. That's it. If 30 minutes a week is too much, this probably isn't the right fit - and that's okay."

### "Why should I help you build your product?"
**Response**: "You're not helping us build our product - you're getting a product built specifically for your workflow. Most tools are built by engineers guessing what agencies need. This is the opposite. You get lifetime pricing locked in, and a tool that actually fits how you work. The question is whether the time investment is worth that outcome for you."

### "What if you shut down or pivot again?"
**Response**: "Valid concern. The infrastructure is built and working - this isn't a prototype. We're not pivoting the product, we're building the right interface for agencies. If it doesn't work out, you've paid a discounted rate for a few months. If it does, you've locked in lifetime pricing on a tool built for you. The downside is limited."

### Standard Objections

### "We prefer human-only work"
**Response**: "I understand. Berlin actually enhances human expertise rather than replacing it. Our AI handles the research and prioritization, but humans make the final decisions and handle execution. Think of it as giving your team a very smart research assistant."

### "Pricing concerns"
**Response**: "I get it. Let's look at it this way - if Berlin saves your team 15 hours per week per client, and your team costs $50/hour, that's $750 in savings per client per week. The ROI becomes clear pretty quickly."

### "Already have tools"
**Response**: "That makes sense. Most of our clients were using multiple tools before Berlin. The difference is integration - instead of jumping between 5 different tools, Berlin brings it all together and tells you exactly what to do next."

### "How do you ensure representative analysis?"
**Response**: "Great question. We don't rely on single queries. We analyze thousands of variations across different contexts, languages, and query types. Our measurement focuses on brand authority patterns that remain consistent regardless of query phrasing. Think of it like taking a statistically significant sample rather than a single data point."

### "Can I just try it and cancel if it doesn't work?"
**Response**: "Absolutely, we're month-to-month. But here's what I recommend to make sure you actually see results: Let's agree on 2-3 specific metrics you care about - maybe branded search visibility and competitor comparison. I'll set those up in week one, and we'll check progress at week 4. That way you're not guessing whether it's working. Does that make sense?"
**Why this works**: Acknowledges flexibility while building commitment through clear success criteria

### "Do you have a trial / free trial?"
**Response**: "We don't do free trials because SEO results take time to materialize - a 7-day trial wouldn't show you real value. But we're month-to-month with no lock-in. Here's what I recommend instead: Let's define 2-3 success metrics upfront, and at week 4 we'll review together. If you're not seeing the trajectory we agreed on, you can cancel. Fair?"

**Never say**: "We don't offer trials because of expenses" - sounds uncertain and cost-focused rather than value-focused

**Why this works**:
- Reframes trial as inadequate for their goals (not about your costs)
- Offers month-to-month as alternative flexibility
- Builds commitment through success criteria
- Sounds confident, not defensive

## Critical Mistakes to Avoid

### Call Structure & Opening
❌ **No redirect when prospect asks direct opening question** - When prospect says "Tell me about Berlin" before discovery, don't launch into pitch. Redirect: "Great question - let me ask a few things first so I can tailor my answer to your situation. Fair?"

**Note on agendas**: For early exploratory/founding partner calls, an explicit verbal agenda can feel overly formal. Having an internal structure you follow is sufficient. Formal agendas matter more for later-stage sales calls with multiple stakeholders.
❌ **Jumping straight into technical topics** - Don't discuss your blog, your tech stack, or features before understanding their needs
❌ **Scattered conversation flow** - Follow the structure: Opening → Discovery → Demo → Close

### Discovery & Qualification
❌ **Asking questions randomly** - Must be systematic: Current state → Pain points → Qualification → Decision criteria
❌ **Missing critical qualification** - Always establish: budget range, decision makers, timeline/urgency, success criteria
❌ **Never asking about budget** - You MUST ask about budget or current SEO spend. Don't skip this because it feels awkward. Simple ask: "What's your current monthly spend on SEO tools or services?" or "What budget range are you working with for this?"
❌ **Skipping "why now?"** - Never leave a call without understanding what's driving urgency (or lack thereof)
❌ **Not qualifying proxy buyers** - When someone says "I'm not the main owner/decision maker," immediately establish:
  - Their role in the decision process
  - How to involve the actual decision maker
  - Whether they have budget authority
  - Offer to include the owner in the call or schedule a follow-up with them

**Note**: Decision maker info often comes from prior context (LinkedIn, email thread, earlier message). Don't assume it wasn't gathered just because it wasn't asked on the call.

### Positioning & Demo
❌ **Over-explaining architecture** - Brief context about why the current UI was built for a different use case is fine and honest. But don't then spend 10 minutes on entity graphs, nodes, and high-dimensional reasoning. The "why" can be one sentence; the demo should focus on "what it does for you."
❌ **Demo without checking comprehension** - If you're explaining something complex, pause and check: "Does that make sense so far?" Don't plow through 10 minutes of explanation only to discover they're lost.
❌ **Not pivoting when pain doesn't match capabilities** - When prospect surfaces a pain you can't solve yet (e.g., backlinks), don't dwell or probe deeply - that just highlights a gap. Pivot quickly: "I hear you - [pain] is brutal. We're building toward that. Where Berlin really shines right now is [what you CAN do]. What's your current process for that?"
❌ **Talking about Berlin's internal journey** - Prospect doesn't care about your agency→SaaS transition, your outsourcing, or your LLM experiments. Never say "we both come from background of building SaaS" or "we started building with the same problem"
❌ **Volunteering uncertainty unprompted** - Don't say "we have two agencies on pilot" or "it's not bad but we're building that out" when the prospect didn't ask. This gives them reasons to hesitate. Instead, frame collaboratively: "We work with agencies and we're actively partnering with agency teams to refine the experience." If they ask directly about a missing feature, be honest - but don't volunteer incompleteness unprompted
❌ **Educational SEO lectures** - Don't explain how crawl budgets work, how LLM algorithms change, or why competitors have more pages. They want results, not lessons. Keep explanations to 1-2 sentences max, then pivot to "here's what this means for you"
❌ **Too much feature focus** - Lead with business outcomes ("save 15 hours/week") not capabilities ("80 signals analyzed")
❌ **Peer conversation trap** - When prospect is technical, easy to stay in technical discussion instead of business value
❌ **Showing uncertainty about your own processes** - Don't say "we're still figuring out pricing" or "we're also early stage with uncertainty"
❌ **Mentioning irrelevant market context** - Never mention "I only work with US clients" or different market pricing when talking to prospects in other regions. Stay focused on their context
❌ **Vague timeline answers** - Never say "compound effects" or "thousand variables" when asked about results timeline. Be concrete: "Month 1: X. Month 3: Y% improvement. Here's why..."

### Pricing
❌ **Pricing confusion** - Don't mention multiple price points without context or explain complex points systems
❌ **Pricing hesitation** - Be confident. Know your value proposition and ROI story cold
❌ **No ROI connection** - Every price mention must connect to value: "For $999, you get X which saves you Y"
❌ **"We haven't figured it out" framing** - When pricing is genuinely being designed (e.g., for new ICP), don't say "we haven't ironed it out yet." Instead: "We're designing agency pricing right now - founding partners help shape that. Current thinking is around $X-Y, but I'd love your input on what makes sense for your workflow." Same information, but positions them as co-designers rather than witnesses to your confusion.

### Closing
❌ **Vague follow-up** - "I'll send an email" or "Let me know when you're ready" is not a close
❌ **No specific next steps** - Must have: specific date, specific action, specific commitment
❌ **Weak response to "can I try and cancel?"** - Don't just say "yes" - use it to build commitment with success metrics
❌ **Letting prospect defer to another meeting without you** - When prospect says "we'll have a separate meeting tomorrow" or "next step is call with founder/marketing team," insert yourself: "I'd love to join that call to answer questions directly. What time works?" or "Can we schedule a 15-minute follow-up with [decision maker] this week?"
❌ **Ending with "that's the next step" without scheduling it** - Don't just identify the next step, book it on the call. "Great, let's get that on the calendar now - does Tuesday or Wednesday work better?"

### Answer Discipline
❌ **Over-answering questions** - When prospect asks a question, answer it directly and stop. Don't volunteer extra context they didn't ask for. If they want more, they'll ask.
❌ **Sharing competitor landscape** - Never say "we have 164 competitors" or "the space is exploding." This signals market chaos and undermines confidence. Instead, differentiate on capabilities: "We're the only ones doing X, Y, Z."
❌ **Price increase without urgency conversion** - Mentioning a price increase is fine, but only if you convert it to urgency with a CTA. Don't just state "price is going up because our costs are high." Instead: "We're increasing to $1,299 next month. If you start before [date], you lock in $999. Want me to hold that for you?"
❌ **Sharing internal journey unprompted** - Don't initiate "let me give you a quick overview of how we got here." They don't care about your agency→SaaS transition or LLM experiments. Start with what Berlin does for them.

### Demo & Assets
❌ **No sanitized demo environment** - Need clean examples that aren't client-specific
❌ **Demo without context** - Never show features before understanding their specific needs
❌ **Poor screen quality without backup** - Always test screen share before the call. Have backup options ready (pre-recorded video, slides, or static screenshots) if bandwidth is poor. If prospect says "it's blurry," switch to backup immediately rather than hoping it improves.
❌ **No comprehension checks during demo** - Stop every 2-3 minutes and ask "Does this make sense?" or "How does this compare to how you do it today?" If they're lost, simplify immediately.

### If They Push Back on Price
1. **Don't discount** - Undermines value
2. **Reframe to ROI** - "Let's look at what you're getting..."
3. **Offer proof point** - "Our clients typically save 15 hours/week, which at $50/hour is..."
4. **Confirm fit first** - "Before we discuss pricing, let me make sure this is the right fit..."

### Using Price Increases as Urgency
If a price increase is genuinely planned, use it to create urgency - but always with a CTA:
- "We're moving to $1,299 next month as we add more automation. Start before [specific date] and you lock in $999. Should I hold that rate for you?"
- "Current pricing is $999. That's going up on [date]. If timing works on your end, starting this week locks you in."

**Never**: Mention price increases as a statement without action ("price is probably going up because it's expensive for us")
**Always**: Frame as benefit to them (lock in lower rate) + specific deadline + clear ask

### Never Say
❌ "We're still figuring out pricing"
❌ "We're early stage so..."
❌ "We have 164 competitors" / "The space is exploding"
❌ "We stopped tracking competitors because there are too many"
❌ "It's becoming very expensive for us" (justifying price with your costs)
❌ Any statement that suggests uncertainty or market chaos

## What Good Discovery Looks Like

### The Discovery Arc (15 mins)

**Phase 1: Current State (5 mins)**
- Their current SEO approach
- Tools they're using
- Team structure
- What's working, what's not

**Phase 2: Pain Points (5 mins)**
- Biggest bottleneck/challenge
- Impact on business (revenue, capacity, growth)
- What happens if they don't solve this
- What they've tried before

**Phase 3: Qualification (3 mins)**
- Budget range or current SEO spend
- Decision makers involved
- Timeline for decision
- Success criteria (what good looks like)

**Phase 4: Urgency (2 mins)**
- "What's driving you to solve this now vs 6 months from now?"
- Listen for: investor pressure, growth targets, competitor threats, new opportunity

### Red Flags - When to Disqualify
- No budget allocated or realistic budget range
- No clear pain point or urgency
- Wrong persona (individual contributor with no budget authority)
- Unrealistic expectations ("I want #1 ranking in 2 weeks")
