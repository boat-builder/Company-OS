The Problem

The pain is not only too many tools. It is fragmented data, brittle automation, rising AI-search complexity, and the inability to scale SEO work without headcount.

Workflow sprawl

5-10 disconnected tools per team

Ahrefs, Semrush, GSC, GA4, spreadsheets, crawlers, and point tools have no unified data layer and no automation capability without developers.

Automation dead-end

n8n / Make still require technical skill

Teams must wire direct APIs or many MCPs, deal with authentication complexity, and absorb model hallucinations across fragmented data flows.

AI search shift

Visibility crisis

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews create new visibility and reporting requirements that legacy SEO workflows do not address well.

Who experiences the problem?

  • Primary: SEO agencies with 1-50+ clients trying to scale delivery without proportional headcount growth.
  • Secondary: In-house marketing and SEO teams at Series B-C SaaS companies and mid-market businesses that struggle to justify SEO budget, attribute SEO to pipeline, and operate without dev support.
  • Emerging: E-commerce and marketplace SEO teams managing thousands of pages, plus growth engineers replacing brittle custom scripts.

How teams cope today

  • Pay $500-$2,000+ per month for multiple disconnected tools.
  • Manually stitch data together in spreadsheets.
  • Build fragile internal automations in n8n, custom scripts, or direct APIs like DataForSEO.
  • Hire more people to handle growing SEO workload.
  • Ignore AI search entirely or improvise with clients.
  • Use Claude Code or similar LLM setups with MCPs but automate unreliably because of hallucination and orchestration issues.
15-20 hrs Manual SEO research and strategy per week per client for agencies.
High churn risk Agencies lose trust when they cannot explain AI search or articulate a clear AI-visibility service line.
Budget at risk In-house teams often cannot prove SEO to pipeline attribution internally.
Wrong actions Automation failures and hallucinations can go unnoticed and trigger bad decisions.
Additional operating pressure SEO and AEO change quickly. Teams constantly face new reports, monitoring needs, and execution motions; when the workflow burden spikes, they either ignore the new landscape or handle it badly.

The Solution

Berlin combines a proprietary and integrated data foundation with a workflow marketplace, custom no-code orchestration, and LLM interoperability.

How the platform is structured

1

Integrated data engine

Keyword intelligence sourced from providers like Semrush and DataForSEO and cached in Berlin's own database, plus proprietary site crawlers and competitor monitoring.

2

Unified access + action layer

Connect once to GSC, GA4, Bing Webmaster Tools, CMS platforms, social accounts, and more. Berlin handles auth, normalization, and shared access.

3

Agentic workflows

No-code, chat-based workflow builder plus a marketplace of pre-built workflows for complex multi-step SEO operations.

4

LLM interoperability + governance

Same infrastructure exposed inside Claude, ChatGPT, and other LLM apps, with brand context, review center, sharing, scheduling, and team governance.

What Berlin has built

  • Integrated SEO data engine with keyword intelligence, proprietary crawlers, and competitor monitoring.
  • Unified data access and action layer that connects analytics, search, CMS, social, and action endpoints.
  • Agentic workflow builder for natural-language workflow creation without code.
  • Agentic workflow marketplace: pre-built, vetted workflows that orchestrate retrieval, analysis, LLM reasoning, and action-taking.
  • Expanding integration layer aiming to become a consolidated agentic system covering data sources, CMS platforms, social channels, indexing APIs, and more.
  • Organizational intelligence: brand context memory, team management, and governance.

How it works in plain language

  • Users connect their data sources and action endpoints once.
  • Berlin normalizes those systems alongside cached keyword data and proprietary crawl data into one queryable and actionable layer.
  • Users either run a pre-built workflow from the marketplace or describe their need in natural language and Berlin generates a structured custom workflow.
  • Workflows can run on demand, on schedule, be shared across the org, and be governed with human-in-the-loop review.
  • The exact same capabilities remain accessible inside Claude, ChatGPT, or other LLM environments through Berlin's tool layer.

Business Model

Berlin is monetized as subscription software with usage-based credits, and it is deliberately underpriced during PMF exploration to maximize learning velocity.

Revenue model

Subscription + credits

Base tier includes workflow execution credits. Additional credits are available on-demand. LLM compute is passed through at cost, and users can bring their own LLM via Berlin's interoperability layer.

Contract value

Current vs. target ACV

Current PMF pricing implies ~$1,200 yearly ACV. Target ACV after pricing maturity is $24K-$60K per year for agency and enterprise accounts.

Gross margin

~80% infrastructure margin

Core platform infrastructure carries ~80% gross margin. Berlin-provided LLM tokens are zero-margin by design to maintain pricing parity with native LLM apps and remove adoption friction; BYO-LLM usage removes token cost from Berlin's P&L and improves blended margin toward infrastructure levels.

Tier Price What's Included Purpose
Free / Explorer $0 Connect domain, run pre-built simple workflows, explore the unified data layer. PLG top-of-funnel; let teams experience the platform before committing.
Pro $100/month Full workflow execution including premium workflows, custom workflow creation, multi-client and multi-project support, base credit allocation. Core self-serve tier for PMF validation.
Agency / Enterprise Custom Higher credit allocations, unlimited automation, priority support, and Forward Deployed Marketers who help set up and optimize workflows on site. High-touch tier for agencies and enterprise teams with complex needs.

Pricing evolution

Current $100/month pricing is intentionally flat during PMF exploration so every feature stays accessible. As product-market fit solidifies, Berlin expects to move multi-client and multi-project capabilities plus premium automation into higher tiers and use per-client or per-project add-on pricing to drive expansion revenue.

Why Now?

The timing case rests on a simultaneous platform shift in search, a workflow shift in AI tooling, and enough protocol maturity to make external LLM interoperability viable.

Macro shift

AI-powered search changed discovery

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews are reshaping how users find information, creating a GEO/AEO visibility crisis that traditional SEO stacks were not built to solve.

Technical enabler

LLMs became usable for workflows

Claude and ChatGPT now support complex multi-step orchestration through natural language with enough reliability to be operationally useful.

Protocol enabler

MCP and tool-use standards matured

Interoperability with external LLM environments has become infrastructure rather than a hack.

Why this was impractical 2-3 years ago

  • LLMs were not reliable enough for structured, multi-step SEO execution.
  • There was no standardized protocol such as MCP for exposing tool capabilities into external AI environments.
  • AI search was too nascent; agencies and in-house teams were not yet panicking about visibility in ChatGPT or Perplexity.

Tailwinds accelerating adoption

  • Agencies are losing pitches and clients to competitors with an “AI SEO” story.
  • CTR drops and SERP volatility are increasing reporting and explanation workload.
  • The SEO tool market ($7B+) is ripe for platform consolidation as teams fatigue from 5-10 subscriptions.
  • AI referral traffic is growing +620% YoY with much higher conversion rates, forcing demand for GEO/AEO capabilities.
  • The GEO services market alone may reach $17-33B by the early 2030s from roughly $1B today.
