## Key Pain Points

- **Managing SEO at thousands-of-pages scale is a constant operational burden.** Category pages, product pages, faceted navigation, seasonal content — all of it needs to be monitored, updated, and optimized. Manual processes don't work at this scale.
- **AI shopping and discovery is changing the purchase journey in ways they can't measure.** Buyers are using ChatGPT or Perplexity to shortlist products and brands. Whether this e-commerce brand appears — and how — is invisible to them with current tooling.
- **Programmatic SEO at scale requires developer involvement they often can't access.** Building and maintaining templates for category pages, generating product content at scale, automating internal linking — these are developer tasks, and the developer queue is always long.
- **Competitor monitoring is expensive and hard to keep fresh.** In competitive category-heavy e-commerce, knowing what competitors are ranking for, what content they're creating, and what their site structure looks like is strategic intelligence. But maintaining crawls across multiple competitor sites is expensive and usually stale.
- **Product content inconsistency across large inventories is an SEO and brand problem.** Different product descriptions, inconsistent category narratives, mismatched terminology across thousands of SKUs creates both a quality and a ranking problem.
- **Attribution for SEO in e-commerce is complex and often undervalued.** With Google Shopping, paid search, and AI Overviews all competing for the same queries, showing the organic contribution clearly is hard — and easy for stakeholders to dismiss.

---

## Core Value Proposition

The platform provides the scale infrastructure that SEO-led e-commerce teams need: always-fresh site crawls and competitor monitoring across large inventories, built-in keyword intelligence without a separate subscription, and a workflow builder that can operate at category and SKU level without developer involvement. Brand context at scale means product descriptions and category narratives stay consistent across thousands of pages — something impossible to maintain manually. AI visibility monitoring gives them a new competitive intelligence layer: which brands (including them) are appearing in AI-assisted shopping queries, and for which categories. And programmatic workflow capabilities mean they can finally execute at the scale their inventory demands.

**In short:** the infrastructure to run SEO at inventory scale — and the visibility to compete in AI-assisted shopping.

---

## Assumptions About Their Priorities

- **Scale is a table-stakes requirement.** If the platform can't handle crawling and querying thousands of pages reliably, the evaluation ends immediately.
- **Revenue attribution is the internal metric that matters.** They need to show that SEO contributes to revenue, not just traffic — especially as paid channels get more credit by default.
- **Competitor intelligence is a strategic priority, not a nice-to-have.** Knowing which category pages competitors own, what they're ranking for, and how their content is structured informs product and content decisions at the category level.
- **Programmatic SEO is either already a core practice or a core aspiration.** The ICP who buys into this platform is the one who's already thinking about SEO as infrastructure, not as a monthly content calendar.
- **They evaluate cost in terms of cost-per-page or cost-per-workflow vs. manual effort.** At 100,000 SKUs, even a small efficiency gain per page has a large aggregate value.
- **D2C brands that are still primarily ad-led are not this ICP.** The qualifier is: SEO-first or programmatic SEO-first orientation. Without that, the value prop doesn't land.

---

## Ideal Roles

- **Head of SEO / SEO Director** — Owns organic performance at scale; decision-maker on SEO infrastructure.
- **E-Commerce SEO Manager** — Specializes in product and category page optimization; evaluates on scale and automation.
- **Category Manager (with SEO mandate)** — May own category page performance; secondary stakeholder.
- **VP of Digital Marketing** — Executive sponsor; cares about revenue attribution and competitive positioning.
- **Programmatic SEO Lead** — Where the role exists, this is the primary champion; deeply technical evaluator.
- **Head of Merchandising (SEO-aware)** — In some orgs, merchandising and SEO overlap on category strategy; potential ally.
