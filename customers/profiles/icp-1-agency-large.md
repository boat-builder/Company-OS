## Key Pain Points

- **Reporting has exploded in volume and complexity.** Clients are panicking about CTR drops and SERP volatility. Every account now demands more explanation, more data, more reassurance — and there's no scalable way to produce that manually across 10–50 clients.
- **They're being asked about AI search and don't have good answers.** GEO/AEO is new territory. Clients are asking "are we visible in ChatGPT?" and most agencies are either winging it or ignoring the question. They need a structured, repeatable way to address it.
- **No developer access to build automations.** They know what they want to automate (rank checks, content briefs, internal link audits, client reports) but they can't build it. They're stuck in spreadsheets and manual exports.
- **Managing data across 10–50 client accounts is a coordination nightmare.** GSC, GA4, rank data, and crawl data all live in different places. There's no single query layer. Every report is a bespoke manual job.
- **Team consistency breaks at scale.** Different team members produce outputs that look and sound different. Without a shared source of truth (brand context per client), quality control is an ongoing problem.
- **Competitive pressure is mounting.** Other agencies are pitching "AI SEO" capabilities. The agencies who can't operationalize it will lose clients to ones who can.

---

## Core Value Proposition

The platform lets agencies build once and run across all clients — without hiring developers. The workflow builder replaces the manual, per-client work that's eating their team's time. The unified data layer (GSC + GA4 + keyword data + crawl + competitor data) means any workflow has access to everything it needs, across every client account. Brand context per project keeps outputs consistent regardless of who on the team runs a workflow. And the GEO/AEO angle is a genuine differentiator they can sell: they can show clients AI visibility data and deliver structured answers, not just organic rankings.

**In short:** more clients, same team size, better deliverables, a new service line to sell.

---

## Assumptions About Their Priorities

- **Retaining existing clients is more urgent than acquiring new ones.** Churn risk is high when clients feel their agency doesn't understand AI search.
- **They are willing to pay for tools that reduce headcount pressure.** Cost is a secondary concern if the tool replaces 2–3 hours per client per week.
- **FOMO is a real purchasing driver.** They've seen peers positioning around GEO/AEO and feel behind. Speed to a credible offering matters.
- **Multi-client management must be native.** A tool that works per-account but doesn't scale across accounts will not stick.
- **They want to look sophisticated to clients**, not just work faster internally. Client-facing outputs (reports, audits, AI visibility dashboards) are as important as internal efficiency.

---

## Ideal Roles

- **Agency Owner / Managing Director** — Decision-maker on tooling and positioning; feels competitive pressure directly.
- **Director of SEO / Head of SEO** — Owns delivery quality and team efficiency; will champion tools that reduce reporting burden.
- **Head of Operations / COO** — Cares about margin, utilization, and scaling without proportional headcount growth.
- **Account Director / Client Success Lead** — Responsible for retention; motivated by anything that improves client-facing deliverables.
- **Senior SEO Strategist** — Hands-on user who will evaluate whether workflows actually save time.
