## Key Pain Points

- **They are doing the job of three people.** Technical SEO, content strategy, reporting, link building, competitive research — one or two people are responsible for all of it, and the surface area keeps expanding.
- **They never get developer time.** Any task that requires engineering involvement (schema at scale, log file analysis, programmatic pages, crawl automation) simply doesn't get done. They've stopped asking.
- **Their tooling budget is tight.** Paying for multiple tool subscriptions (keyword research, crawling, reporting, rank tracking) is hard to justify when headcount is also squeezed.
- **AI search is a major stressor.** They know things are changing. They're reading about GEO, AEO, AI Overviews, zero-click queries. But they have no time to learn a new framework, let alone implement one.
- **Reporting to leadership is painful.** Founders and execs want to know "is SEO working?" and the honest answer — "it's complicated right now because of AI" — is not one they have confidence delivering with data to back it up.
- **They feel behind.** Larger companies have teams, developers, and tools. A solo SEO at a startup feels the gap growing every quarter.

---

## Core Value Proposition

For a solo or small-team SEO, this platform replaces several tool subscriptions with one — keyword data, crawling, GSC/GA4, and more are all built in. More importantly, the workflow builder gives them the developer access they've never had: they can describe what they need and get a repeatable, schedulable workflow without writing code. The LLM integration is a particularly strong hook here — if they're already using Claude or ChatGPT daily (which they almost certainly are), they can now bring their full SEO data stack into those conversations. Brand context means they produce consistent outputs even without a team around them. And pre-built workflows mean they don't have to start from scratch — they can start from something sensible and customize it.

**In short:** one tool that replaces many, and finally gives you the automation you've been asking engineering for.

---

## Assumptions About Their Priorities

- **Time is the scarcest resource.** ROI is measured in hours saved per week. If the platform saves them 5 hours of manual work, it's worth it.
- **They are already heavy AI tool users.** They use Claude or ChatGPT every day. LLM integration is not a novelty — it's how they want to work.
- **Cost consolidation is a meaningful buying signal.** If the platform can replace 2–3 other subscriptions, the math becomes much easier to justify internally.
- **They need fast wins to justify the tool to their company.** If they can't produce something useful in the first week, the tool will be abandoned. Pre-built workflows and templates are critical to onboarding success.
- **They're not evaluating enterprise features.** Team management, org-level controls — these are secondary. What matters is: can one person use this to do more?

---

## Ideal Roles

- **SEO Manager** — Often the only SEO person; buyer, user, and champion in one.
- **Growth Marketing Manager** — SEO is part of a broader mandate; values tools that consolidate workflows.
- **Content Marketing Manager** — May own SEO by default; needs accessible automation, not technical depth.
- **Head of Marketing (at early-stage startup)** — Wears many hats including SEO; time-starved decision-maker.
- **Founder / Co-Founder** — At very early stages, may be doing SEO themselves; extremely time-constrained.
